How Cybersecurity Started? The Creeper Experiment (1970s): Bob Thomas, an ARPANET developer, created a program called Creeper. It wasn't malicious, but it highlighted the possibility of programs moving across a network, raising concerns about potential security vulnerabilities. This playful experiment is often seen as the first spark of cybersecurity awareness.
The 1990s and Beyond: The internet's explosive growth brought a surge in cybercrime. Hackers targeted businesses and individuals, and the need for robust security solutions became ever more critical. Firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and encryption technologies became commonplace.
Cybersecurity is now a complex and ever-evolving field. Here's a glimpse into the current landscape: Cybercriminals are constantly developing new tools and techniques. Phishing attacks, malware, ransomware, and data breaches are just some of the ongoing threats organizations and individuals face.
